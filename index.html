Trusting God Deeply: Growing in Surrender, Dependence, and Confidence

How to Use These Guidelines

Sabbath teaches us that we are not ultimately sustained by our own strength, control, or constant effort. It invites us to trust that God is present, faithful, and at work even when we stop striving.

Yet trusting God deeply is not always easy. We may carry fears about the future, unresolved burdens, disappointments, or pressure to hold everything together ourselves.

This season is an invitation to practice surrender:

  • Release control gently
  • Bring your fears honestly before God
  • Rest in His faithfulness
  • Learn to depend on His care

"Trust in the Lord with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding."
— Proverbs 3:5

Before

  • Notice areas of life where you feel the need to control outcomes.
  • Pray: "Lord, help me loosen my grip and trust You."
  • Acknowledge your limitations honestly.

During

  • Read Proverbs 3:5-6 slowly.
  • Whenever anxious thoughts arise, pause and surrender them to God.
  • Resist the urge to manage or solve everything today.
  • Spend time resting in silence or prayer.
  • Practice saying: "God is with me, even when I do not control everything."

After

  • Reflect: What situations are hardest for me to surrender? How did letting go affect my peace today?
  • Entrust your concerns to God again before ending your Sabbath.
  • Rest in His care.

Before

  • Begin your Sabbath acknowledging your need for God.
  • Pray: "Lord, I cannot sustain myself apart from You."
  • Release the pressure to appear strong or self-sufficient.

During

  • Read John 15:4-5 slowly.
  • Spend several moments asking: "What would it mean to depend on God right now?"
  • Allow yourself to rest physically and emotionally.
  • Receive help, kindness, or encouragement freely if it comes.
  • Practice gratitude for God's sustaining presence.

After

  • Reflect: Where do I struggle to depend on God? What reminded me of God's care today?
  • Thank God for carrying you through each day.
  • End your Sabbath with quiet trust.

  • Bring your uncertainties and unanswered questions before God.
  • Pray: "Lord, help me trust You with what I cannot yet see."
  • Write down any fears about the future.

During

  • Read Jeremiah 29:11-13 slowly.
  • Resist dwelling on worst-case scenarios.
  • Whenever fear arises, breathe deeply and return your attention to God's presence.
  • Spend time doing something peaceful and grounding.
  • Remember that God's faithfulness extends beyond your understanding.

After

  • Offer your future to God once again.
  • Rest in the assurance of His presence.

  • Reflect on times God has sustained you in the past.
  • Pray: "Lord, help me remember Your faithfulness."

During

  • Read Lamentations 3:21-26 slowly.
  • Spend time recalling moments of God's provision, guidance, or comfort.

After
