Resting in God

Trusting God Deeply  ·  Surrender · Dependence · Confidence

"Trust in the Lord with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding."

— Proverbs 3:5

Welcome to Season 7

Sabbath teaches us that we are not ultimately sustained by our own strength, control, or constant effort. It invites us to trust that God is present, faithful, and at work — even when we stop striving.

Yet trusting God deeply is not always easy. We may carry fears about the future, unresolved burdens, disappointments, or pressure to hold everything together ourselves.

This season is an invitation to practice surrender.

Release control gently.
Bring your fears honestly before God.
Rest in His faithfulness.
Learn to depend on His care.

Trust grows slowly through repeated moments of surrender. Each Sabbath Day in this season contains three sections to guide your practice:

🌿

Before

Prepare your heart by becoming aware of the burdens or fears you may be carrying into the Sabbath.

☀️

During

Practice rhythms of surrender, dependence, peace, and trust in God's care throughout your day.

🌙

After

Reflect on how God met you through rest and trust, and record your personal reflections.

✉️

Reflect & Share

Each day includes an option to email your personal reflections to yourself or someone you trust.

This is Day 31 through Day 35 — the seven Sabbath Days of Season 7. Move through each one at your own pace, trusting that God is present in every moment of your rest.

Day 31 · Season 7
Proverbs 3:5–6

"Trust in the Lord with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding. In all your ways acknowledge him, and he will make straight your paths."

🌿

Before

  • Notice areas of life where you feel the need to control outcomes.
  • Acknowledge your limitations honestly.
"Lord, help me loosen my grip and trust You."
☀️

During

  • Read Proverbs 3:5–6 slowly.
  • Whenever anxious thoughts arise, pause and surrender them to God.
  • Resist the urge to manage or solve everything today.
  • Spend time resting in silence or prayer.
"God is with me, even when I do not control everything."
🌙

After

  • Entrust your concerns to God again before ending your Sabbath.
  • Rest in His care.

Day 32 · Season 7
John 15:4–5

"Abide in me, and I in you. As the branch cannot bear fruit by itself, unless it abides in the vine, neither can you, unless you abide in me. I am the vine; you are the branches."

🌿

Before

  • Begin your Sabbath acknowledging your need for God.
  • Release the pressure to appear strong or self-sufficient.
"Lord, I cannot sustain myself apart from You."
☀️

During

  • Read John 15:4–5 slowly.
  • Throughout the day, ask: "What would it mean to depend on God right now?"
  • Allow yourself to rest physically and emotionally.
  • Receive help, kindness, or encouragement freely if it comes.
  • Practice gratitude for God's sustaining presence.
🌙

After

  • Thank God for carrying you through each day.
  • End your Sabbath with quiet trust.

Day 33 · Season 7
Jeremiah 29:11–13

"For I know the plans I have for you, declares the Lord, plans for welfare and not for evil, to give you a future and a hope. Then you will call upon me and come and pray to me, and I will hear you."

🌿

Before

  • Bring your uncertainties and unanswered questions before God.
  • Write down any fears about the future.
"Lord, help me trust You with what I cannot yet see."
☀️

During

  • Read Jeremiah 29:11–13 slowly.
  • Resist dwelling on worst-case scenarios.
  • Whenever fear arises, breathe deeply and return your attention to God's presence.
  • Spend time doing something peaceful and grounding.
  • Remember that God's faithfulness extends beyond your understanding.
🌙

After

  • Offer your future to God once again.
  • Rest in the assurance of His presence.

Day 34 · Season 7
Lamentations 3:21–26

"But this I call to mind, and therefore I have hope: The steadfast love of the Lord never ceases; his mercies never come to an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness."

🌿

Before

  • Reflect on times God has sustained you in the past.
  • Enter your Sabbath with gratitude rather than fear.
"Lord, help me remember Your faithfulness."
☀️

During

  • Read Lamentations 3:21–26 slowly.
  • Spend time recalling moments of God's provision, guidance, or comfort.
  • Write down several ways God has cared for you through the years.
  • Allow remembrance to strengthen your trust.
  • Rest peacefully in the knowledge that God remains faithful.
🌙

After

  • Thank God specifically for His care throughout your life.
  • Close your Sabbath with worship or quiet gratitude.

Day 35 · Season 7
Psalm 55:22

"Cast your burden on the Lord, and he will sustain you; he will never permit the righteous to be moved."

🌿

Before

  • Identify the emotional or spiritual burdens weighing most heavily on you.
  • Release the pressure to carry everything alone.
"Lord, I bring these burdens to You."
☀️

During

  • Read Psalm 55:22 slowly.
  • Spend time in quiet prayer, surrendering your concerns one by one.
  • Imagine placing your burdens into God's hands.
  • Rest physically if your body feels tired.
  • Allow yourself to receive God's peace without striving.
🌙

After

  • Thank God for His sustaining strength.
  • End your Sabbath with a prayer of trust and release.
